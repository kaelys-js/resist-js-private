Browned Butter Icing

Ingredients (Serves 2)

Icing Base
• 85 g (6 tbsp) unsalted butter
• 120 g (1 cup) powdered sugar
• 30 ml (2 tbsp) whole milk
• 2.5 ml (½ tsp) vanilla extract
• 0.5 g (⅛ tsp) fine sea salt

⸻

Method
	1.	Brown the Butter
Melt butter in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, stirring continuously, until the aroma turns nutty and the milk solids become golden brown with amber flecks. Do not let it turn dark or smell acrid.
	2.	Stop the Cooking
Immediately remove from heat and pour the brown butter into a heatproof bowl to halt further browning. Let rest 3 minutes until very warm but no longer sizzling.
	3.	Build the Icing
Whisk powdered sugar into the warm brown butter until thick, smooth, and glossy, ensuring no dry pockets remain.
	4.	Finish and Adjust
Whisk in milk, vanilla, and salt until silky and pourable. The icing should ribbon smoothly off the whisk. If too thick, add milk a few milliliters at a time; if split, warm gently and re-whisk.

⸻

Optional Refinements
• Rest the brown butter 5 minutes before mixing to deepen nutty aromatics
• Substitute heavy cream for milk for a richer, more luxurious texture
• Use warm on cookies or cakes for best adhesion and sheen
